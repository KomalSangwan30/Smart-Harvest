Know Your Crops

🌾 Rice

Kharif

The most important food crop in India. It grows in flooded fields and loves warm, humid weather. More than half the world eats rice as their main food.

Temperature20 – 35°C
Rainfall150 – 300 mm
Soil pH5.5 – 7.0
Water NeedVery High 💧

🌽 Maize

Kharif

Also called corn. It grows fast and gives high yield. Used for eating, animal feed, and making biofuel. Very popular among farmers across India.

Temperature18 – 35°C
Rainfall60 – 120 mm
Soil pH5.5 – 7.5
Water NeedMedium 💧

🥭 Mango

Summer

The king of fruits in India! It loves hot weather when flowering. India is the world's biggest producer of mangoes. Needs dry weather before harvest.

Temperature24 – 40°C
Rainfall90 – 200 mm
Soil pH5.5 – 7.5
Water NeedMedium 💧

🌸 Cotton

Kharif

Called "White Gold" by farmers. It is used to make cloth and is the most important cash crop in India. Needs black soil and lots of sunlight.

📅 Planting Guide

When to Grow What?

India has 3 main farming seasons. Choosing the right season for your crop is one of the most important decisions a farmer makes.

☀️

Kharif Season

JUNE TO NOVEMBER

Sown when the monsoon starts, harvested before winter. These crops love heat and heavy rain.

🌾 Rice 🌽 Maize 🌸 Cotton 🌿 Jute 🍌 Banana
❄️

Rabi Season

OCTOBER TO MARCH

Grown in the cool winter months after the monsoon. Needs irrigation since rainfall is low during this time.

🫘 Chickpea 🫘 Lentil 🍇 Grapes 🍎 Apple 🍊 Orange
🌸

Zaid Season

MARCH TO JUNE

A short season between Rabi and Kharif. Crops that grow fast and do well in hot, dry summer conditions.

🍉 Watermelon 🍈 Muskmelon 🥭 Mango 🍑 Papaya

💡 Smart Farming

Useful Tips for Every Farmer

Small things that can make a big difference to your harvest

💧

Drip Irrigation Saves Water

Using drip irrigation instead of flood irrigation can save up to 50% water. The water goes straight to the roots, and there are fewer weeds growing too.

🔄

Rotate Your Crops Every Season

Never grow the same crop in the same field twice in a row. Rotating crops stops pests from building up and keeps the soil naturally healthy without extra fertiliser.

🌡️

Check Soil Temperature Before Sowing

Seeds only sprout when the soil is warm enough — around 15 to 25°C for most crops. Planting in cold soil means seeds rot or don't grow properly.

🍂

Add Compost to Improve Soil

Even a thin layer of compost on your field can make a big difference. It improves water holding, adds nutrients slowly, and feeds the tiny organisms that keep your soil alive.

🐛

Use Less Pesticide When Possible

Try neem oil or natural methods first before using chemical sprays. This saves money and does not damage the soil for your next crop season.

🧪

Test Your Soil Regularly

Getting a soil test done every 2 to 3 years tells you exactly what nutrients are missing. Applying fertiliser without knowing what you need is just wasting money.
